## Design Context

### Users
General consumers across all demographics — anyone managing personal budgets and tracking expenses. They want to feel in control of their money without needing financial expertise.

### Brand Personality
**Premium, precise, elegant.** The interface should feel like a well-crafted financial tool — trustworthy, refined, and efficient. Think Monarch Money or Copilot — warm, inviting, but with meticulous polish.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Dark-mode-first with warm charcoal tones (`#212121`) and orange/copper brand accent (`#e78a53`)
- Subtle shadows, rounded UI, smooth transitions
- Reference: Monarch Money, Copilot Finance
- Anti-reference: AI-generated dashboard slop (no glowing gradients, no cyan-on-dark, no generic glassmorphism everywhere)
- Must NOT look like it was generated by AI — should feel intentionally designed

### Design Principles
1. **Warmth over coldness** — Finance is stressful; the UI should feel approachable, not clinical
2. **Precision in every pixel** — Spacing, typography, and color must feel deliberate and cohesive
3. **Confidence through clarity** — Users should always know where they are and what to do next
4. **Restraint over excess** — Avoid decoration for decoration's sake; every element earns its place
5. **Distinctive, not generic** — Must pass the "would you believe AI made this?" test
